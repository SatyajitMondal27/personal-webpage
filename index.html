Section 1: About Me

I am a graduate student with a strong interest in how people collaborate using technology. I am taking this class to better understand communication and coordination software, both from a technical and a human-centered perspective. I am especially interested in how these tools affect teamwork, productivity, and decision-making in academic and professional settings.

Section 2: Collaborative Software I Use

  • WhatsApp Staying in touch with friends and family through text, voice notes, and group chats.
  • Discord: Communicating while gaming online seeing if friends are playing the same game I am playing.
  • Zoom / Google Meet: Attending online classes, meetings, and group discussions.
  • Google Calendar: Scheduling meetings, setting reminders, and coordinating availability.
  • Google Docs: Collaborative writing, editing documents in real time, and working on shared assignments.
  • GitHub: Coordinating software projects, version control, and collaborating on code with others.

Section 3: Helpful Features and Areas for Improvement

The most helpful features of these tools for me would be real-time collaboration, notifications, and cross-platform . Features like live editing in Google Docs, screen sharing in Zoom, and issue tracking in GitHub make it much easier to work together efficiently.

However, these tools could be improved by improving integration across platforms, and offering better support for accessibility. Sometimes, switching between many different apps can be distracting, and a more unified experience would help streamline collaboration.
